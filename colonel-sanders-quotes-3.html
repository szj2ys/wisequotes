110 Colonel Sanders Quotes

[On whether he spoiled his grandchildren] Not too badly. I just love them.
Colonel Sanders

You’re surrounded with love when you’re surrounded with a bunch of children.
Colonel Sanders

[On children] They’re an inspiration for us all to grow by. And you can’t help but admire them.
Colonel Sanders

[On being called a living legend.] A legend, and what’s that?
Colonel Sanders

I ran a restaurant for twenty-five years trying to serve the best food I could up to the public.
Colonel Sanders

I wanted to have the best chicken in my restaurant.
Colonel Sanders

They forgot all about the price of the food… They’d come back again for that good food.
Colonel Sanders

I had a moral obligation to give people something good. Something worthwhile. I loved it.
Colonel Sanders

[On hearing about the new interstate highway coming] I thought what can I do next?
Colonel Sanders

[On the new interstate highway being put in and the impact on his original restaurant] I was left high and dry.
Colonel Sanders



[On the new interstate highway forcing him to change and adapt] It’s a stepping stone for something else, there’s no question about it.
Colonel Sanders

So my question was ‘What could I save out of that wreck?’ I knew it was going to be wrecked.
Colonel Sanders

I think the moral for my life is don’t quit at 65. Maybe your boat hasn’t come in yet.
Colonel Sanders

I prayed to god that he would make this thing a success.
Colonel Sanders

My ferry boat business was put out of business when a bridge was built across the river.
Colonel Sanders

My lighting plant business selling gas lights went downhill when there were electric lights and I couldn’t sell mine.
Colonel Sanders

[On previous business failures – ferry boat business (failing when a bridge was built across the river), lighting plant business etc] Things just went bad for me all the way along… I always dreamed up something that I thought was useful and good.
Colonel Sanders

I wanted to be sure on this chicken as it was my last chance. Cause I was getting well up in my years. If this chicken made good I’d see that God got his part. I’ve given away millions of dollars since then…
Colonel Sanders

I don’t pray for anything…My prays are to thank for the all the blessings I’ve received over the years. Here I am enjoying good health. My eyesight is getting bad now and my hearing is failing a little bit.
Colonel Sanders

Don’t be against things so much as for things.
Colonel Sanders



[On being asked at close to his 90th birthday - ‘Don’t you believe in retirement?’] No, not a bit in the world. When the lord put old father Adam here he never told him to quit at 65 did he?
Colonel Sanders

There’s some mighty fine railroad men but I wasn’t one of them.
Colonel Sanders

[On Salvation army group people never paying at his restaurant] Nobody ever paid me for anything. I figured I could afford…
Colonel Sanders

I got $2 million dollars when I sold out… and when they sold out seven and a half years later they got as of now $273 million dollars. So that’s quite an appreciation in value you see.
Colonel Sanders

[In 1979] Sales this year will exceed $2 billion dollars worth of chicken.
Colonel Sanders

[On his chicken being cooked by other KFC stores and some people trying to change the formula or stretching the spices by adding more flour] It just had to be right, that’s all there was to it.
Colonel Sanders

[On adding more flour to the secret KFC chicken recipe to make it go further for less money] He could stretch it but he depreciated the value of my product.
Colonel Sanders

[On giving a band free KFC chicken in return for them playing music in the front of his store] Come back anytime on the same deal.
Colonel Sanders

I made a resolve then that I was going to amount to something if I could. And no hours, nor amount of labor, nor amount of money would deter me from giving the best that there was in me. And I have done that ever since, and I win by it. I know.
Colonel Sanders

I couldn’t afford to quit.
Colonel Sanders
