There is no finish line.
Robert Herjavec

Is earning a business degree an assurance of success for entrepreneurs? Not really.
Robert Herjavec

You don’t bite the hand that feeds you.
Robert Herjavec

Do not assume that every business idea you have is worth a million dollars on its own. Because it is not.
Robert Herjavec

Academics… may be good at delivering theory, but theory itself has no value…
Robert Herjavec

Theory itself has no value, and using it as a weapon to shoot down practical experience is unacceptable.
Robert Herjavec

Robert Herjavec

To become successful entrepreneurs: a clear vision, a viable opportunity, an effective business model, a determination to succeed…
Robert Herjavec

Investors – can be as fleeting as a leaf in the wind if things don’t go well.
Robert Herjavec

The true motivation for becoming an entrepreneur in the first place: the overwhelming, often obsessive desire to bring a vision to life. This desire is so powerful that it cannot be diverted or diluted even by an awareness that the odds of success are against it, and it is not enhanced with dreams of enormous wealth that the venture may generate.
Robert Herjavec



No true entrepreneur seriously regrets the sacrifice made to realize his or her dream.
Robert Herjavec

No matter how badly conceived and poorly presented their ventures may be, I admire them for their determination to take risks and their preference to play the role of entrepreneur over that of employee.
Robert Herjavec

Abrasive, or just honest?
Robert Herjavec

Every now and then in your life as you try and chase your dream you get to a crossroad where you have to make a decision, which way are you going to go?
Robert Herjavec

Isn’t that really what you’re selling today? You’re selling yourself.
Robert Herjavec

I’m Croatian. It’s a very melodramatic story, I came to Canada when I was eight years old, literally on a boat. My grandmother raised me on a farm where we had dirt floors and horses and everything. And I didn’t realize I was poor and when we landed in Canada I got of the boat and I said ‘Wow, we’re really poor.’
Robert Herjavec

I took over newspapers when I was 12 years old.
Robert Herjavec

My first business that I got a job in I convinced the guy to hire me even though I had no sales experience, and no technology experience. I worked for free. So I got the job, I left and then I realized ‘How am I going to pay my rent?’ So I got a job waitering.
Robert Herjavec

Absolute commitment. I would do anything to succeed, as long as it was legal.
Robert Herjavec

I love the idea. But I had to admit it - I’m a pretty old boring guy. I don’t really hang out at night clubs.
Robert Herjavec



[On selling his first company for a $100 million dollars.] It’s a lot of zero’s.
Robert Herjavec

[On ‘overnight success’ and how long it took him to build up his company where he was able to sell it for $100 million.] It took me nine years to get to that first one. It’s funny I sold the next business for $230 million and it only took me 16 months. So I made more money in the next 16 months than I did in the ten years before.
Robert Herjavec

I think everybody wants to take care of the ones they love. I think everybody wants to make more money. Everyone wants to take care of their family. I think people are afraid. I think fear holds people back. I meet a lot of people who say, ‘Oh money’s not important to me.’ And I say ‘That is absolute crap. I remember when my mum and dad didn’t have enough money to put food on the table. Today we fly around the world in a private jet. My kids will never be hungry. How is that not better?’
Robert Herjavec

I feel blessed to be alive. I love it. I have a great life. I mean, I have absolutely nothing to complain about.
Robert Herjavec

I’m as happy now as when I had nothing.
Robert Herjavec

[On having said ‘I’m as happy now as when I had nothing.’] It’s true.
Robert Herjavec

I’m the same passionate, fun loving, motivated guy as before. It was never about the money for me, it was about being successful. I think as an immigrant you always have a chip on your shoulder. You always want to prove yourself. And I think making some money validated my existence for me.
Robert Herjavec

[On having said ‘There’s no magic in success.’] There’s no magic.
Robert Herjavec

Hard work.
Robert Herjavec

I think it’s hard work and working smart. I mean I know a lot of people who worked hard and never made any money. My dad was a blue collar factory guy who worked his butt off and never made any money. So I think you’ve got to work hard, but you have to work smart.
Robert Herjavec
