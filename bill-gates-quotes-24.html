Knowing your numbers can help you transform all of your business relationships and give you a significant competitive advantage.
Bill Gates

The business side of any company starts and ends with hard-core analysis of its numbers.
Bill Gates

If you don’t understand what’s happening in your business factually and you’re making business decisions based on anecdotal data or gut instinct alone, you’ll eventually pay a big price.
Bill Gates

Numbers give you the factual basis for the directions in which you take your products. Numbers tell you in objective terms what customers like and don’t like. Numbers help you identify your highest priorities so that you can take fast tactical or strategic action.
Bill Gates

You have to think, act, evaluate, adapt.
Bill Gates

[In 1999] Just-in-time delivery can be a reality for any industry.
Bill Gates

Everyone who touches the product must add value…
Bill Gates

You need to step back and come up with a new approach.
Bill Gates

The inevitable consequence of better computer systems is a smarter use of people’s time.
Bill Gates

What digital tools do that paper reports cannot is give everyone the ability to ask the next question. Because you never know what that question is going to be, you need tools that help you explore the answers on your own.
Bill Gates



What distinguishes my satisfied customers from my unsatisfied customers?
Bill Gates

[In 1999] Targeted ads should make consumers happy. They’re more likely to see ads that are relevant to them.
Bill Gates

Soap operas – the staple of daytime TV in the United States – are so named because the advertisers were traditionally the big soap companies going after the audience of largely female viewers.
Bill Gates

The secret to success with information products is understanding the profile and buying habits of your most likely customer.
Bill Gates

You always have to ask what’s actionable.
Bill Gates

The better tools they have, the more creative they can be.
Bill Gates

As software extracts more and more ore from the mine of information, people will always have work turning it into gold.
Bill Gates

Knowledge management is nothing more than managing information flow, getting the right information to the people who need it so that they can act on it quickly.
Bill Gates

To recruit and retain smart people, you need to make it easy for them to collaborate with other smart people.
Bill Gates

Intellectual capital is the intrinsic value of the intellectual property of your company and the knowledge your people have.
Bill Gates



Knowledge management is a fancy term for a simple idea. You’re managing data, documents and people’s efforts.
Bill Gates

If you decline to take risks early, you’ll decline in the market later. If you bet big, though, only a few of these risks have to succeed to provide for your future.
Bill Gates

Risk taking is natural in an emerging industry.
Bill Gates

If you’re going to go digital, you have to go digital all the way.
Bill Gates

If the basic operational processes of your business fail, your company fails.
Bill Gates

If you don’t believe that all workers have the potential to contribute to your company’s success, all the technology in the world won’t empower them. Once you assume that every employee should be a knowledge worker, technology will help every employee put his or her full abilities to work on the company’s behalf.
Bill Gates

Computers will eliminate some jobs, but they will take the drudgery out of many other jobs.
Bill Gates

If you cut a job into too many pieces and involve too many people, nobody can see the whole process and the work will bog down.
Bill Gates

Every project has trade-offs.
Bill Gates

You need to be flexible in the face of evolving requirements, without invalidating your original design goals with creeping change.
Bill Gates



If you find more than three or four decision makers in the room, you can be sure that the sheer number of people involved is a major part of the problem.
Bill Gates

Shipping is a feature.
Bill Gates

Nobody can predict every possible wrinkle or problem with a new process or application.
Bill Gates

Bill Gates
