One of the secrets of legendary NBA coach Pat Riley’s success is his commitment to gradual, consistent improvement. In 1986 he was faced with a major challenge: his team thought they’d played their best, but the championship had still eluded them the year before. To inspire them to move to the next level, he convinced them that if each enhanced they performance by a mere one percent in five key areas, it would make all the difference. The genius of this plan was it’s simplicity: everyone felt certain they could achieve it. Each player had only to dedicate himself to a five percent increase, but multiplied by twelve players, it produced a sixty percent improvement for the team – their best season ever.
Anthony Robbins
