Elizabeth Arden

Elizabeth Arden

Elizabeth Arden

[In 1959 when she was told one of her racehorses had bitten off the tip of a person’s finger] What happened to the horse?
Elizabeth Arden

[On passing by a limestone façade bearing the words ‘Helena Rubinstein’ after she had passed three weeks earlier at the age of 94] Poor Helena. [Elizabeth herself passed away just 18 months later at the age of 81]
Elizabeth Arden



When people think pink… they think Arden.
Elizabeth Arden

[At a political fundraiser towards the end of the 1930s] You don’t have to do anything, my dears – just vote Republican.
Elizabeth Arden

Hanque, I’m tired of Miss What’s-her-name’s copy. I think you ought to fire her and get someone else. [He would murmur agreement and do nothing and then when he next presented ‘Miss What’s-her-names copy] I like these words much better. [And then a few months later] You know, Hanque, I think I preferred Miss What’s-her-name’s copy. Perhaps you’d better hire her back. [This went on for years and no one ever dared tell her the truth.]
Elizabeth Arden

[She had] Given her life to the study of her subject in America [In response to Helena Rubinstein text saying she had ‘Given her life to the study of her subject in America.’]
Elizabeth Arden

Elizabeth Arden
