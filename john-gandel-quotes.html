100 John Gandel Quotes (Chadstone Shopping Centre Quotes)

1 2 3



In business, you need to be lucky, you have to work hard and you always take a very conservative approach.
John Gandel

Persistence is the key to any success.
John Gandel

[Three principals he lives by which has led to his success.] The first is to seize the moment. Second, is the recognition of an unusual opportunity and the final one is to action a carefully thought out plan.
John Gandel

We have a family philosophy that is partly derived from our Jewish heritage, which by law recommends that everyone should give 10 % of what they earn to charity.
John Gandel

We’ve been successful, but I always say you need timing and good luck to stay stable and increase your net worth.
John Gandel

For me, giving is an obligation. It’s part of our philosophy, our way of life.
John Gandel

I think we’ve been fortunate and hope to help those we can.
John Gandel

[At the age of 77] We work not to make more money.
John Gandel

[At age 77 in 2012] I have no plans to retire.
John Gandel

We’ve always made giving a priority…
John Gandel



I watched as our house burnt down… It was very emotional.
John Gandel

We relate to the pain and loss of the victims of Black Saturday.
John Gandel

[On his house burning down] We tried to salvage what we could. We paid large dry-cleaning bills to try and save some clothes but they were all ruined and smelt of fire.
John Gandel

We’d never experienced fire before. I couldn’t believe how quickly it destroys.
John Gandel

My parents always donated money for various philanthropic causes.
John Gandel

Everyone should give 10 % of what they earn to charity…
John Gandel

[On the Carbon tax] The tax comes at an inappropriate time.
John Gandel

The government could easily take another two to three years to get the budget into surplus…
John Gandel

We may well suffer from the introduction of the carbon tax…
John Gandel

[In 2010] I don’t think anybody knows how long the journey to recovery will be.
John Gandel



We have been around for a while and have not had any financial disasters.
John Gandel

[In 2012] We anticipated the global financial crisis and slowdown.
John Gandel

I have no idea how much I’m worth, but… if there’s only one bid of $10.
John Gandel

Hope I won’t be sitting down to lunch with five tenants…
John Gandel

We looked at each other and knew we had to do something immediate and substantial.
John Gandel

We felt if we came forward it might enthuse other families and corporations.
John Gandel

We didn’t want publicity but we wanted others to join us in supporting these victims.
John Gandel

We wanted our money to go where it was needed…
John Gandel

I watched as our house burnt down.
John Gandel

[On philanthropy] It is part of returning something back to the community…
John Gandel



We have given substantial amounts of money to charity.
John Gandel

You need to be lucky…
John Gandel

You have to work hard…
John Gandel

You always take a very conservative approach.
John Gandel

I hope that our support will inspire others…
John Gandel

[In 2012 at the age of 77] We have to make ourselves relevant and keep all our faculties going.
John Gandel

[In 2010] There’s a greater need for charity now more than ever.
John Gandel

We give as we see fit.
John Gandel

I always say you need timing and good luck to stay stable and increase your net worth.
John Gandel

We are traditional and not religious…
John Gandel
