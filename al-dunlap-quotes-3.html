To placate everybody, consensus managers sacrifice what’s important. On the other hand, strong managers willing to risk failure and criticism will make very different decisions. Better decisions. And their companies get their total commitment to carrying them through.
Al Dunlap

If you operate by consensus, you’re not making hard decisions. Instead, you’re looking for something easier, something less.
Al Dunlap

Take responsibility, make decisions, take risks! No matter how you carve it up, that’s what management is all about. Anything less is just seat-warming.
Al Dunlap

This is the easiest money you will ever make, like taking candy from a baby: 1) Leverage volume as much as possible 2) Narrow suppliers to a handful 3) Encourage multiple vendors to bid on requirements 4) Negotiate hard contracts 5) Practice brinkmanship. Let vendors know that they don’t have an eternal lock on your business 6) Significantly challenge the procurement organization to take cost out and support them in doing so 7) Ask vendors to reduce costs by utilizing their own expertise and resources.
Al Dunlap

Everything that costs money is negotiable…
Al Dunlap

Negotiations are negotiations; there is always a certain amount of b_s_ on the table.
Al Dunlap

Change is a good thing.
Al Dunlap

[In 1996] You can outsource just about anything today.
Al Dunlap

Even if you have the best damn payroll system in the world, it does not give you a competitive advantage in the marketplace.
Al Dunlap

You should only do, in-house, what gives you a competitive advantage.
Al Dunlap



Personnel or human resources groups are a crutch between management and the workforce. Woe unto the consultant not wise enough to realize that.
Al Dunlap

What never ceases to amaze me is how some executives still get away with spending shareholder money on themselves as if they were kings and queens and the business was their royal domain.
Al Dunlap

I cause people to achieve more than they ever thought they could achieve.
Al Dunlap

Your ideas will never see the light of day unless you communicate.
Al Dunlap

Once a final decision is made, it’s time to stop talking and start implementing.
Al Dunlap

A company is not your high school or college alma mater. Don’t get emotional about it.
Al Dunlap

Remember that the shareholder is king.
Al Dunlap

If we don’t make money, we won’t last another day in business.
Al Dunlap

Where others see the impossible, I see opportunity.
Al Dunlap

Take responsibility, make decisions, take risks!
Al Dunlap



The process is painful. Sometimes, it’s ugly. But in the end, it’s worth it.
Al Dunlap
