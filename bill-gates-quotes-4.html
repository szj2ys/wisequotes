Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

Bill Gates

The mind has a lot of unused bandwidth.
Bill Gates

[On Windows in October 1984] It will be a significant advancement of the state of the art.
Bill Gates

[On developers at Microsoft being reviewed every six months by their peers and those in the bottom five percent being fired.] There are other jobs out there. If they don’t have what it takes to work at Microsoft, they can go to Boeing or back East.
Bill Gates

Being successful is not a fun thing sometimes. There is just a phenomenon where people don’t like a company as successful as ours.
Bill Gates

Ask any Wall Street analyst which company never over promises what’s going to happen, always talks up the risks in their business and takes the long-term approach. It’s Microsoft.
Bill Gates

I have an infinite amount of money. I would still order the same hamburger.
Bill Gates

I’ll ship my stuff before they ship theirs.
Bill Gates

[When running late for a meeting at a downtown Sheraton Hotel with friend Heidi Roizen suggesting they use the hotel’s valet parking.] Yeah, but it’s $12 and that’s not good value. [‘I’ll pay the $12.’ – Heidi Roizen] That’s not the point. They overcharge for parking.
Bill Gates

[In the early 90’s] Computers are not very easy to use. We don’t have information at our fingertips.
Bill Gates



There is one thing that is fun – I look out there and see fun people to work with, who are learning a lot. That’s cool, and that feels good…
Bill Gates

[At the 25th anniversary of the University of Washington’s computer science department] We’re scared all the time. We’re always saying, ‘Is this the day we’ve reached our peak?
Bill Gates

Most business problems are information problems…
Bill Gates

Do we practice what we preach?
Bill Gates

A bad strategy will fail no matter how good your information is.
Bill Gates

An old business joke says that if the railroads had understood they were in the transportation business instead of the steel-rail business, we’d all be flying on Union Pacific Airlines.
Bill Gates

An unsuccessful maker of Japan’s first electric rice cooker became Sony Corporation…
Bill Gates

A company should not take its position in the market for granted. A company should constantly re-evaluate.
Bill Gates

Information is thinking work.
Bill Gates

[In 1999] We need to break out of the mind-set that getting information and moving information around is difficult and expensive.
Bill Gates



People like knowing that something they’re doing is working…
Bill Gates

Bill Gates
