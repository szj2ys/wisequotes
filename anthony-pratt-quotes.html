Anthony Pratt Quotes

I like to do things that other people say can’t be done.
Anthony Pratt

Business is about playing both pedals and having your fingers on the keys.
Anthony Pratt

The art of business is always cut and grow, cut and grow.
Anthony Pratt

I am a builder by nature.
Anthony Pratt

The most underestimated thing in business is management continuity.
Anthony Pratt

I have a philosophy that your best people should always be at the very top…
Anthony Pratt

I like to do things that you need to chip away at.
Anthony Pratt

Business in the US is war…
Anthony Pratt

America is a huge market where persistence is very important…
Anthony Pratt

I did want to go to America to prove myself.
Anthony Pratt



I’ve always believed it was better to be a baron in your own fiefdom than a lapdog in the palace.
Anthony Pratt

Business is a passionate enterprise…
Anthony Pratt

[To his father Richard Pratt over a business decision] I’m the chief executive of this operation, not you!
Anthony Pratt

I will never voluntarily go public. [Listing Visy on the stock market]
Anthony Pratt

[In 2011 on what would be one piece of advice for his son Leon] Stay healthy. That is all that matters.
Anthony Pratt

Nobody really knows the answer to what will happen in the future…
Anthony Pratt

I think climate change can be solved by engineers.
Anthony Pratt

I believe in the difference that a man or woman can make at the top of an organization.
Anthony Pratt

Once you have a really good guy in place at the top, the next most important thing is continuity of management, as opposed to a revolving door.
Anthony Pratt

[In March 2013 on Ashok Jacob.] Ashok has joined us. I feel very fortunate about that. He is a great addition to the board. Apart from being very smart, he obviously knows a lot about India. It might be important going forward, without giving away what we are going to do
Anthony Pratt



[In March 2013 on Ashok Jacob] He has been a great friend to me and is a terrific bloke.
Anthony Pratt

[On the Pratt Family Board] Not a statutory board of directors per se. It is a board of advice. So it is a bit different to a public company in that respect. It meets a few times a year.
Anthony Pratt

[On John Wheeler taking over as Chief Operating Officer] At the time that we announced John's appointment, we also announced Brian McPheely was going to become the global CEO at a date to be determined. We did it because it was sensible for succession reasons. The day the CEO goes in is the day you plan for the next one. I have a philosophy that your best people should always be at the very top, whether it is in a board or management. I have been grooming Brian for this job since 2009 and the company now has more global aspirations than it ever did.
Anthony Pratt

[In March 2013 on John Wheeler] John has been great. He has grown the sales and cut the costs. He has done a great job at both.
Anthony Pratt

[On John Wheeler] He is a tough guy. He is an extraordinarily good thinker, a great executor, a great manager.
Anthony Pratt

Screwing down costs in procurement has been a very good source of cost improvements. Productivity improvement is always important and a good job has been done on that. And the art of business is always cut and grow, cut and grow.
Anthony Pratt

[In 2009] My father Richard Pratt was many things to many people. Business leader… environmentalist.
Anthony Pratt

[On his father Richard Pratt] To me he was simply my hero.
Anthony Pratt

[On Richard Pratt] The man taught through his actions.
Anthony Pratt

Richard [Pratt] always said family came first, but work was second. Not third or fourth.
Anthony Pratt



An immigrant who came to this country with nothing. A four year old polish refugee who couldn’t even speak English until he was five.
Anthony Pratt

Hard work, determination and vision, helped him to achieve so much…
Anthony Pratt

He built Australia’s greatest environmental company. My father was a true renaissance man.
Anthony Pratt

Richard [Pratt] gave more to philanthropic causes, created more jobs, and did more for the environment than just about any Australian.
Anthony Pratt

[If a person’s greatness is measured by his philanthropic endeavours.] My father was a giant among men.
Anthony Pratt

He was a great father to all of his children. Always there when needed. Always there to offer advice. Always there when the going got tough.
Anthony Pratt

Both my mother and father – were and are great parents.
Anthony Pratt

Even though he’s gone now, Richard [Pratt] will live on for decades – decades to come in his achievements, dreams and lives of his grandchildren.
Anthony Pratt

Be strong, be honest – work hard. This was my father, this was my hero.
Anthony Pratt

[In 2011] I want to bring Leon and Lilly up in Australia. At the moment, I spend six weeks in Australia and two weeks in the US and when Leon starts going to school in 18 months or two years, Claudine and I will be permanently based in Australia. They will continue to travel overseas but that will be where we live.
Anthony Pratt
