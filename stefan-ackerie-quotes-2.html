[On his purchase of Brisbane’s Skyneedle from Expo 88] Now that’s one hell of a story. I had a phonecall one day from a person and he wouldn’t stop calling or tell the girl on the switchboard what it was about. Finally he told her it was about the Skyneedle. I said what about it? He said if somebody doesn’t buy it by Friday – this was Tuesday – it was being sold to Tokyo. So I said OK, you’d better come in and we’ll talk. I had been in Europe in that period of time and I saw a lot of monuments and lots of landmarks and symbols that represented great moments, for example the Eiffel Tower in Paris. That was an Expo symbol and they were going to tear it down. What they wanted to do to the Skyneedle is kind of the same thing. It’s a 10 million dollar piece of art and they want to tear it down and sell it to Tokyo. Then I started to get quite upset, because where I live I see it every night, so I became quite emotional and attached to it. It was a very special monument for Brisbane, and I got caught and thought how dare they sell this to Japan. So I got a bit carried away and I did it. The Skyneedle is the highest symbol in Australia, it is 300ft, and it’s the biggest piece of art in Australia. Forget the money issue, you need to have history.
Stefan Ackerie

[In June 2009] MY greatest achievement in life has been finding Queensland. I remember so clearly the day I drove into Brisbane. I had lived in Adelaide, Melbourne and Sydney, but about five minutes after I arrived here, I knew this was where I wanted to spend the rest of my life. My memory is so clear of that first day - the sky was blue and everything was smiling at me and I thought, ‘This is where I belong’. I've travelled the world three or four times a year, and in my opinion nothing compares. Nothing compares to the lifestyle we have, the food we have, the beaches we have, the golf courses we have and the choices we have. People can go where they choose, to the country or the beach, and you don't have to be rich. You don't have to pay to go to these places. I am proud of my business on many levels. I started out in Longreach before opening a salon in Maryborough, Mackay, and then in Adelaide St in Brisbane. To have in excess of 500 people helping you on your journey and seeing how they develop is very satisfying. I am constantly getting letters from my staff who have gone on with their careers, saying thank you for my help. It is an amazing thing when you are lucky to help so many young people, and the end result is that they are helping you on your journey. The Skyneedle is also one of my greatest achievements. After the Paris World Expo, half the city wanted The Eiffel Tower torn down and the other half didn't and then it was put in the too hard basket. Now it is the greatest icon in the world. Well, Brisbane's Skyneedle was going to be sold to Japan, or to Tokyo's Disneyland. I feel very lucky and very privileged to have kept it in Brisbane. People stop me in the street and offer the most amazing compliments. I had a police officer stop me once and I thought, ‘What have I done’', but he just shook my hand and said, ‘Congratulations for giving Brisbane a heartbeat’. The Skyneedle is an amazing thing. I love Queensland. When you are walking down the street, people will stop and say hello. I think we should never forget our duty and our commitment. We should contribute to the place where we live and contribute to the people we come in contact with. It's no different to a garden. If you don't contribute to a garden, it won't grow. If you don't contribute to the moment, it won't grow. I live in Queensland, and I love to contribute to this great state.
Stefan Ackerie
