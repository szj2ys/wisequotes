Two years later I got fired… and I didn’t care to railroad anymore.
Colonel Sanders

[On his first restaurant] I feed truck drivers, millionaires all at the same table. I didn’t know anything about the restaurant business. I didn’t know what top of the hold, a la carte or anything like that meant. We would cook meals for our family but wouldn’t eat right away ourselves… sometimes we would sell [the meals] all out, sometimes we didn’t.
Colonel Sanders

Where either you’re on your own job or you’re dreaming about different things and you can always take something in your life and make it better.
Colonel Sanders

We know we’ve got a good thing… They just think it is the chicken but it’s got to be like Kentucky Fried Chicken if it’s going to win it’s way in the world.
Colonel Sanders

When Jesus took the cussing out I lost 50% of my vocabulary.
Colonel Sanders

I was born on the farm. I got a job working on the farm…
Colonel Sanders

I was 5 years old when Papa died…
Colonel Sanders

My mother was a poor widow woman on the farm. She made dresses for the girls… She would direct me on what to do with the food she had on the stove – what to add… so I came up a cooking just as a kid.
Colonel Sanders

By the time I was 7 years old I was making a family lightbread.
Colonel Sanders

I was 7. My sister was 5 years younger, she was 2.
Colonel Sanders



I guess that’s the reason why I finally fell back on the restaurant business, I knew something about food and how to handle it. That’s where I got the idea… of developing my chicken recipe.
Colonel Sanders

[On the highway interstate coming through, cutting traffic dramatically away from his restaurant] The question was what can I save out of this wreck? People liked my chicken …
Colonel Sanders

[On naming KFC Kentucky Fried Chicken] I wanted to be called Kentucky Fried Chicken… Because I didn’t want to be Southern Fried… because it was wasn’t Southern Fried. So I called mine Kentucky Fried. Kentucky is a wonderful name anyway.
Colonel Sanders

[On his old restaurant after the new highway interstate was put through seven miles away from his store taking traffic with it before founding KFC] I sold it at auction. I just got enough out of it to pay my taxes and bills you see… I was 66 years old when I started franchising in earnest.
Colonel Sanders

I was 65 when I had a guinea pig franchise out or two. Knowing that I was going to have to franchise and see how it would take you see. So then I started franchising in earnest when I was 66 years old.
Colonel Sanders

[On how long it took for KFC to take off] It took about two years. I made only about a nickel on every chicken that they sold. And they got three dinners out of it. But I got one nickel for the whole meal. But you’d be surprised at how fast those nickels added up… I finally got up to where I had an income of a thousand dollars a day.
Colonel Sanders

My only reason for selling the company was I didn’t have anybody to succeed me in running it from my wife and two daughters…
Colonel Sanders

I thought that $2 million would last the rest of my life. But it didn’t… I gave my first payment of $50,000 to the Salvation Army…
Colonel Sanders

[In 1979 on KFC stores] We’re in 51 countries around the world with over 7,500 stores.
Colonel Sanders

I gave my [KFC] Canadian franchise to charity… I gave the whole $6 million company to charity… For that reason I’ve got about 1,000 students on scholarships throughout the country in eight different colleges and that’s better than me getting it. Because if I got that income it would go to taxes…
Colonel Sanders



[On the man above] I thought he’d do something for me, and I’m trying to do it.
Colonel Sanders

If I could just make peace with my maker…
Colonel Sanders

I used to curse terrible…
Colonel Sanders

I wanted to quit for years and years, but I couldn’t quit to save my life.
Colonel Sanders

I could do the prettiest curse in the world…
Colonel Sanders

Time is getting shorter all the time for all of us…
Time is getting shorter…for all of us…
Colonel Sanders

When I started this chicken business… I prayed to God that… he’d make it a success… And I hope that I have and am still doing it.
Colonel Sanders

I just don’t know too many 89, 88 year old men travelling a quarter of a million miles a year and doing the work like I always did.
Colonel Sanders

[On being in a car accident and someone saying they would call for a doctor and then proceeded to fix himself] Y’all call a damn doctor when I say so.
Colonel Sanders

[To his daughter Margaret his official taste tester] Here it is if anything should happen to me here is the list of the eleven different herbs and spices.
Colonel Sanders



[On starting out with nothing financial at age 65 except for his cheque from social security for $105 per month] That paid for my gas and the travel needed to get the franchises started. Lots of nights I would sleep in the back of my car so I would have enough money to buy cookers the next day if someone took a franchise.
Colonel Sanders

Nobody else has what we’ve got – the greatest chicken in the world.
Colonel Sanders

Service is the best thing in the world you can give to your customers.
Colonel Sanders

There’s no such thing as bad weather – just different kinds of good weather.
Colonel Sanders

It makes me feel good that my food has helped make life a little easier for people.
Colonel Sanders

I cursed by note.
Colonel Sanders

[On being asked why he was still working at almost 90 years of age] I think as long as you’ve got health and ability why, use it!
Colonel Sanders

It’s all good you know.
Colonel Sanders

You’re once a man and twice a child.
Colonel Sanders

I dearly love children… I have five grandchildren and fourteen great-grandchildren.
