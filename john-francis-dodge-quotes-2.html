We were born out on North Fifth Street in a little wooden cottage, close to where your standpipe is located. In those days we were the most destitute kids in the town. Poor mother, how she used to worry about her boys. I am three years older than my brother and naturally mother always confided her trouble in me. When cold weather came and H. E. and myself were obliged to go barefooted and wear ragged clothes, we didn’t grumble, but tried to make mother think it was all right.
John Francis Dodge

[In August 1913 giving a gift of $100,000 to the city of Niles to build a park honouring their parents] This amount sounds big but really is nothing to us now. H. E. and myself are worth $50 million and we have made the most of it in the last 10 years. We want to do something for Niles right away and I had suggested a donation of $100,000. We will double this amount if your citizens will advance a judicial manner in which it can be spent. [But the city of Niles rejected the gift after its members questioned the need for a park and determined that the city might have to spend money to maintain it.]
John Francis Dodge



[On being told there was only a job for one of them] We’re brothers and we always work together; if you haven’t got room for two of us, neither of us will start. That’s that!
John Francis Dodge

[On providing bicycle riding lessons free for E. & D. Bicycle owners and $2.00 for all others for five lessons] You can learn at the rink without publicity and under the instruction of capable instructors.
John Francis Dodge

[On founding Dodge Bros] Builders of simple, compound, triple and quadruple expansion marine and stationary engines. We also build and repair any kind of machinery, do all kinds of difficult punch, die and tool work; do internal, external and surface grinding, milling, gear cutting, and punch press work; in short, we are prepared to do any class of work that can be done in a first-class modern shop. Linotype machines repaired skillfully and promptly.
John Francis Dodge

[On 26 March 1901 to F. F. Whitney, the general foreman of the Wabash Railway in Ontario] As we understand that you have no repair facilities in Windsor, we would respectfully call your attention to the superior facilities that we possess for doing all kinds of machine repair work.
John Francis Dodge

Trusting that when you receive inquiries for steam launches you will give us a chance to bid on the machinery.
John Francis Dodge

[In July 1901 to various newspapers with outstanding bills] Gentlemen: As we have disposed of our interests in the Typograph (Company), we would be obliged for a prompt settlement of the above.
John Francis Dodge

[In October 1901] We have been asked to figure on a quantity of Automobile Transmission Gears.
John Francis Dodge

[On routinely working until midnight in their business] During the two years we occupied the Boydell Building, I spent a total of only six weekday evenings at home.
John Francis Dodge

[On Kahn’s failure to get the blacksmith shop completed when the weather was good] We are certainly not well pleased with the way you have handled this job and we must insist that our work be given more attention.
John Francis Dodge

[In November 1913 on being passed on a letter from James Couzens a director at the Ford Motor Company for Frank Dodge (no relation)] I should very much like to do something for this young man, but we have found it necessary to establish a rule that no one by the name of DODGE be given employment in our factory. We have had two or three cases and have always had a bad experience in each case. While you will not think it is much of a reason, it really has caused us considerable annoyance and we have decided that two members of the Dodge family are all that the firm should be burdened with.
John Francis Dodge
